Discrete Mathematics - Logical Propositions

Introduction to Logical Propositions

Logical propositions are declarative statements that can be true or false. They form the basis of discrete mathematics and computational logic, often used to evaluate the relationships between variables.

Key Concepts

  • Truth Tables: Evaluate truth values systematically.
  • Logical Connectives: AND (∧), OR (∨), NOT (¬), Implication (→), and Biconditional (↔).
  • Tautologies and Contradictions: Propositions that are always true or always false.
  • Applications: Logic gates, problem-solving, and decision-making.
